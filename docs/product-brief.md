# OpenCue Product Brief

OpenCue is a Mac-native streaming app for solo creators who stream camera and screen together. It is not trying to become full OBS feature parity. The first product promise is simpler:

> Go live. Keep talking. OpenCue cues the stream.

The wedge is a calm director that watches local signals and suggests or performs scene changes. Captions, blur, voice isolation, summaries, and local models support the experience, but they are not the identity.

## First Audience

- coding streams
- product demos
- design streams
- workshops and teaching
- founder livestreams
- screen-share podcasts

## First Workflow

1. Pick camera, mic, screen/window, and RTMP destination.
2. OpenCue creates four scenes: Face, Screen + Face, Screen, and BRB.
3. The user starts streaming.
4. The director watches speech, screen motion, active app, face presence, idle state, and stream health.
5. The app suggests a cue, or switches automatically when the user enables Auto mode.

## Product Principle

Minimal means fewer knobs, not a shallow director. The first app should have a small surface area and one deep behavior: it helps a solo creator avoid babysitting production while live.
