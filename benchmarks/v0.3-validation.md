## Real Publisher Integration

The actual HaishinKit publisher was exercised against MediaMTX on localhost,
with FFmpeg independently reading and validating the published stream:

| Duration | Validation target | Decoded at ingest | Result |
| ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| 5 seconds | 75 frames | 89 H.264 frames | Passed |
| 60 seconds | 900 frames | 899 H.264 frames | Passed |
| 60 minutes | 54,000 frames | 54,014 H.264 frames | Passed |

All runs produced 640x360 H.264 video, reported nonzero publisher outbound
bytes, accepted at least 90% of submitted frames, and drained the publisher
append queue on close. The long run used the Apple hardware encoder on the
release-candidate source. Its paced synthetic generator took longer than 60
minutes of wall time because frame creation is serialized with the cadence
sleep; publishing remained connected through clean shutdown.

Hosted macOS runners cannot initialize the virtualized Apple media encoder.
CI and release automation therefore generate a compressed H.264 fixture and
send its `CMSampleBuffer` values through MacStream's real HaishinKit publisher,
MediaMTX, and FFmpeg ingest path. Local release validation retains the raw-frame
Apple hardware encoder path. This split validates transport in CI without
pretending that a hosted runner tested physical capture or hardware encoding.

## Packaged Capture And Recording QA

The arm64 HaishinKit release package was exercised with the built-in MacBook Pro
Camera, a wireless microphone, and the `Screen + Webcam` scene.

- The idle webcam preview released the camera before the media pipeline started
  its recording capture session.
- Camera frames continued to refresh the composed output when the captured
  screen was static.
- Wireless microphone buffers were normalized to interleaved PCM before being
  appended to the recording writer.
- Recording stopped cleanly and the studio returned to `Ready` without writer
  failure logs.
- Two resulting `.mov` files were validated with FFprobe:

| Duration | Video | Audio | Result |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| 87.47 seconds | H.264, 1920x1080 | AAC stereo, 48 kHz | Passed |
| 118.41 seconds | H.264, 1920x1080 | AAC stereo, 48 kHz | Passed |

Extracted frames showed both the selected screen and the built-in camera in the
recorded composition. This closes the short packaged `Screen + Webcam`
recording blocker. It does not replace the required long-duration RTMP and
record-while-streaming runs.

## Release Decision

The release owner accepted v0.3.0 after the automated matrix, real transport
integration, 60-minute native publisher run, packaged Screen + Webcam recording
checks, UI smoke test, and complete signed/notarized dry run passed.

The following broader scenario tests remain post-v0.3 hardening work and are not
represented as completed by this ledger:

- a 60-minute packaged Screen + Webcam stream with record-while-streaming;
- controlled 5-second and 30-second network interruptions against remote ingest;
- 20 packaged start/stop cycles with physical capture devices;
- clean-user installation, permission recovery, and capture smoke testing.

MacStream remains a personal-use prototype. Important broadcasts should still
be validated against a private or test destination first.
